About Dialysis

Hemodialysis is a medical procedure for individuals with kidney failure. During this process, blood is circulated through a machine that acts as an artificial kidney, removing waste products and excess fluids. A dialyzer, comprising semipermeable membranes, filters toxins from the blood. The machine then returns purified blood to the patient. Typically performed three times a week, each session lasts about 3-5 hours. Hemodialysis helps maintain a balance of electrolytes and fluid in the body, managing blood pressure and preventing complications associated with kidney failure. While it sustains life, a kidney transplant is often considered a more permanent solution for eligible patients.
